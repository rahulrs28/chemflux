Turncoat And the SIX ‘ER’ Hats !!!!.

UK October 3, 2016 Turncoat And the SIX ‘ER’ Hats !!!!.2016-10-03T09:42:35+00:00 General, Hr Library No Comment

Source | LinkedIn : By Adil Malia

In my career as I look back, I figuratively wore ‘6 Different Hats’…one, as a Labour Lawyer….second, as an IR Practitioner (handling Settlements and Strikes with late Dr. Datta Samant)….third as a teacher building law, ER and OD Capabilities  (at The TISS )…fourth, as an operating HR Business Partner in different Companies…fifth as an OD/HR Manager in MNCs….and the sixth as a Strategic Business Leader at Global levels in  large Conglomerates.

Let me therefore examine this theme  : ‘Re-emergence of the White Collared Unionism’ from its various dimensions. The re-emergence for discussion of this so perceived ‘fuddy-duddy’ theme has derived new currency more so in light of the spate of recent ad hoc displacements of white-collared professional work force by several Cos in their life-saving attempt to survive and thrive in the current state of Economic distress.

Let me therefore shuffle and wear each of the above ‘Six Hats’  to dissect this subject and provide it in an all-dimensional perspective.

1. The Legal Hat :

Let us examine the situation by answering the moot legal question in reference : Can white-collared professional employees and Supervisors, legally form themselves into Trade Unions ?

For the uninitiated, the answer to this takes us to provisions of :

?Article 19 (g) to the Constitution of India which deals with the Fundamental right of every citizen of India to form an Association. Association in this context includes a Union. I am de-cluttering interpretations and ratios of a plethora of case laws on this point, for ease of reader understanding. White-collared workmen, supervisors and professional staff have a fundamental right to form an Association (hence a union). Employers will not stand much of a chance or success should they prefer to challenge formation of their Associations by arguing to the effect that such Association of White Collared professional workmen or Supervisors is principally against the interest of the Management’s un frittered right to conduct it’s business and hence its status be struck-off . To form such an association is every citizens Fundamental right – whatever the colour of his collar or for that matter not having a collar at all !!!.

?Like wise, provisions of Sections 2(g) and 2 (h) of The Trade Unions Act, 1926, which defines ‘Trade Dispute’ and ‘Trade Union’ respectively, when interpreted in light of ratios of cases, leaves no doubt that such an Association/Union of white-collared professional jobs holders would find suitable protection under provisions of The TU ACT, 1926.The definition of ‘Workmen’ under the TU Act is much broader and wider than as defined under Sec. 2(s) of the ID Act 1948. Such an association of white collared professional workmen, would therefore get necessary protection under The TU Act. Airline Pilot Guilds are a Union. Bank Officers have a union…and to that extent it is a well settled principle in law that their right to form an Association under the Constitution of India is a  Fundamental Right and that they can also be registered as a Trade Union under the TU Act,1926.
